How The Court Works

This is how the court of NoodleBox works.



Fines:

  • If one is fined any amount, that fine goes towards the court's safe vault.

Suing:

  • If one is deciding to sue, they must pay a fine of 5 iron ingots.
  • If one is being sued, the defendant must pay the fine declared by the plaintiff as soon as they have the resources.
  • If the defendant refuses to pay the fine or cannot pay the fine by 2 real-life days, the plaintiff may take possession of any 1 item/building the defendant owns.
  • The legal amount of times someone can sue is 3 per real-life week (Monday-Sunday).

Court Procedures:

The NoodleBox court must run in a specific way:

  • Plaintiff (accuser) states their terms
  • Defendant (accused) pleads "guilty" or "not guilty"
  • Defendant states their terms
  • Plaintiff must provide evidence
  • Defendant may argue against the evidence
  • Plaintiff may call a witness to the stage
  • Plaintiff asks the witness questions regarding the case
  • Defendant asks the witness questions regarding the case
  • Defendant may call a witness to the stage
  • Defendant asks the witness questions regarding the case
  • Plaintiff asks the witness questions regarding the case
  • The judge (or jury if provided) comes to a decision
  • Case dismissed

Court Rules:

  • If one is being disruptive, the judge may hold them in contempt of court and punish them.
  • Silence must be upheld during a court case.
  • If the judge says "order" or rings the bell, all conversations must stop. If anyone continues, they may be held in contempt of court.

Objections:

  • One can only call an objection if they believe the argument is irrelevant or unruly.
  • When one calls an objection, all arguments must be stopped to hear the objector's reasoning.
  • If the judge believes the reasoning is irrelevant, they may say "overruled", then the argument will continue.
  • If the objection is accepted, the judge may say "sustained", then the argument will pause until the objection is over.
